“Professionals” refer to attempted participants in a capitalist setting in which products or services are offered to customers for monetary exchange.
“Friends” refers to parties with shared values or good will. Some contexts may default to treat strangers as friends, others may require further knowledge of profiles for the designation. In a competitive setting some users may be excluded.
“Researchers” refers to users who wish to follow the scientific method to extend and document knowledge or theory. Prioritization given to reputable academic or professional Affiliations or otherwise seeking to advance the Public Good.  
“People” refers to human beings, as such the art may be expected to draw from human values and forms of expression. 
“Engineers” refers to agents designing, building, repairing, or decommissioning systems to be used by or for human beings. Systems may refer to eg mechanical, electrical, computer, geological, software, or organizational, as typically characterized by defined specifications, assumptions, and a responsible human being.
“Leaders” refers to agents whose actions or responsibilities are likely to impact the lives of other human beings. Prioritization given to increasing scales of possible reach. 
“Players” refers to users participating in competitions. 
“Students” refers to users seeking to build fundamental knowledge or skills. Prioritization given to reputable academic or professional affiliations or otherwise attempting to advance the Public Good. Fundamentals refers to knowledge applicable to the domain of inquiry. More esoteric domains require better defined learning objectives.
“Patients” refers to human beings. Prioritization given by insurance / hospital affiliation or otherwise those seeking to advance the Public Good.
